No one is aware of the meaning or what the hell the group even does, so I thought it best to go over a brief history to celebrate almost a year of Internet Forum. [^1]

## History

Around this time last year, I was on vacation. I was back home in Chicago and I didn't have much to do, and the 6 months were nothing short of Hell. Close friends and family passing, unnecessary drama, everything else that was put in a journal to spare you from reading it on my website. I had the idea down of just having a group that comes together to do little online concerts and things like that, because that's what I'd admired about the underground scenes at first, but it never really came to fruition. The name "Forum" just kinda stuck out to me over that vacation period, so I'd created the account with the intent of it being an anonymous group project, similar to [user-177606668](https://soundcloud.com/user-177606668).

At that time, I was friends with [Matt](https://www.instagram.com/mphe_jzsl6ucrhze/) and other friends that made music. I eventually stopped talking to those other friends as drama ensued that I wasn't involved in and felt too shitty to care about. Matt and I continued to keep contact though, and as weeks passed, I asked them to be apart of it, and I could not be more grateful. They're actually responsible for the button-type graphics on the banner, and part of the management/upkeep of the group page.

Maybe a month passed before I'd invite members [Karsten](https://instagram.com/twilightmirrorz3) (ki) and [Amir](https://instagram.com/smileliketexas) (chirp), who I'd like to thank for finding the audio tag we all use:

Then came Jensen, Irene, Natan, etc, etc. I'd started drawing fursonas of everyone in the group:

[![alt text](../assets/img/forum7.png)](../assets/img/forum7.png)

 We started doing "Forum Fridays," where every first Friday of the month, there'd be a mix of unreleased songs and remixes by us, with maybe like one delay(?) They're all getting decent play, which was (and still is) surprising to me.

[![alt text](../assets/img/forum8.png)](../assets/img/forum8.png)

## Results

Ultimately, I think that this collective was a net positive for everyone. Although at times, it was difficult to balance both this *and* school *and* work, I'm happy I got to work with these people. I'm happy for the side accounts it erected, like elioe39, where we'd all just post throwaways. It allowed me to create a completely new alias and gather love on a seemingly otherworldly level.

[![alt text](../assets/img/forum10.png)](../assets/img/forum10.png)
<sub>[This song](https://soundcloud.com/elioe39/stop_this_madness) getting 2,000 plays is still surreal to me!!</sub>

 I can't speak for any real impact (because there is none, we just make music,) but I'd like to think that making music with (and drawing fursonas of) my friends helped me find better ways to cope and giggle. I hope it was the same for some of them.
