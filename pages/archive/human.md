# How to Dress as Human

![](https://cdn.sonemic.net/i/600/s/b480ec9fbbd6f41b7c221ecdf2ce81b6/8674071/laura-les-how-to-dress-as-human-demo-Cover-Art.jpg)

---

3:30 AM. My brother keeps kicking me in his sleep. I'm listening to a demo from Laura Les that caught my eyes during 2021 when 100 gecs was really really cool. The Obsidian team changed the UI of the app. I'm still not used to change.

*How to Dress as Human* is subtle, yet raw in its delivery. The mildly saturated guitar, with the accompanying vocals, are very calming. I'm not very good at complimenting a song for what it is, but it reminds me a lot of *I Remember You* from Adventure Time in its chord progression and choice of key, and it overall feels like something Rebecca Sugar would've done when she first started her solo music career. Can you count how many comparisons and references I make when talking about music?

As for the lyrics themselves, I had picked up on them only slightly; I was in seventh grade at the time (2021, so some months after the original release date.) Gender and the gender spectrum weren't a new concept, but it wasn't one I was certainly researching. Being on the computer a majority of my time allowed me to be ignorant of people's orientations and preferences to focus on common interests that we shared, and my parents aren't any kind of phobic, so I never had an issue of misunderstanding or attacking what I dont understand. But listening back, it allowed me to reflect a bit.

I've had friends, past and present, who have transitioned and have had their moments. Their moments where they feel like they're never going to pass as the opposite gender, but also moments where they feel *hopeful*. And the conversation was not that different from a conversation I'd have with other people.

And then after a couple of years, I listen to this song and think about all the times I've questioned myself in the mirror.
Questioned if this is how *every* human feels.

And the truth is, yes. Yes, it is. I think we should all be a bit nicer to ourselves and to others.

Yeah the songs fucking fire
